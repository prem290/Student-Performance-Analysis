PRINCIPAL'S REPORT

Executive Summary

The student performance dataset was analyzed to identify factors affecting academic achievement. The analysis found that parental education level and completion of the test preparation course have a positive impact on student performance. Strong positive correlations were observed among Math, Reading, and Writing scores.

Key Findings

1. Students whose parents have higher education generally achieve higher scores.
2. Students who completed the test preparation course scored higher in all three subjects.
3. Reading and Writing scores have a very strong positive correlation.
4. Female students performed better in Reading and Writing, while male students performed slightly better in Mathematics.
5. Students scoring below 50 in any subject were identified as at-risk.

Recommendations

1. Encourage all students to complete the test preparation course before examinations.
2. Provide additional academic support and mentoring for at-risk students.
3. Conduct parent awareness programs to increase family involvement in students' education.
